Introducing yourself/Sich vorstellen

Ich heiße ... - This means My name is ..., heiße is a verb that is used to name something or someone, If your name is Bruno you can say, Ich heiße Bruno for example, there is other 2 ways to say your name, you can say Mein name ist ... which means the same thing as "Ich heiße ...", and the last way you can say introduce yourself is saying Ich bin ..., which means I am, for example you can say Ich bin Bruno.

Mein Familienname ist ... - This means My surname is ..., if your surname is Schwartz, you can say Mein Familienname ist Schwartz for example.

Ich komme aus ... - This means I come from ..., as an example you can say Ich komme aus Deutschland which means I come from Germany.

Ich wohne in ... - This means I live in ..., as an example you can say Ich wohne in München if you live in Munique.

Ich bin 20 Jahre alt. - This means I am 20 years old, obviously you would say your own age, this is only an example.

Ich bin Student/Studentin. - This means I am a student, if your a male you would say Ich bin Student, but if your a female, your would say Ich bin Studentin.

ICh bin Schüler/Schülerin. - This means I am a school student, if you are a male you would say Ich bin Schüler, but if you are a female you would say, Ich bin Schülerin.

Ich bin Lehrer/Lehrerin. - This means I am a professor/teacher, you can also say your own profession, but as the previous sentences you can notice that there is a diference between Lehrer and Lehrerin, the first one is for males and the second one is for females, it will be a diference in your profession too.

Ich bin ledig. - This means I am single.

Ich bin Verheiratet. - This means I am married.

Ich habe Kinder/Ich habe ein Kind. - This means I have children/I have one child, but if you don't have children you can say Ich habe keine Kinder.

Meine Hobbys sind spielen Klavier und Musik hören. - This meansMy hobbys are playing piano and listening to music, you would like to say your hobbys instead of these ones.

Getting to know someone/Jemanden kennenlernen

Something that is important to mention is the formal and informal pronouns, you would use Sie when talking with someone you don't know well, it's basically the formal pronoun, while the pronoun du you would use while talking with friends and family, basically it's the informal pronoun

Wie heißen Sie?/Wie heißt du? - This is how you would ask someone's name, it means What is you name?.

Wie ist Ihr Familienname?/Wie ist dein Familienname? - This is how you ask someone's surname, it means What is your surname?.

Woher kommen Sie?/Woher kommst du? - This is used to ask someone where they come from, it means Where are you from?.

Wo wohnen Sie?/Wo wohnst du? - This is used to ask someone where they live, it means Where do you live?.

Wie alt sind Sie?/Wie alt bist du? - This is used to ask someone's age, it means How old are you?.

Welche Sprachen sn Sie?/Welche Sprachen sprichst du? - This is used to ask someone which languages they speak, it means Which languages do you speak?.

Was machen Sie beruflich?/Was machst du beruflich? - this is used to ask someone what they do for a living, it means What do you do for a living?.

Was sind Ihre Hobbys?/Was sind deine Hobbys? - This is used to ask someone what are their hobbys, it means What are your hobbys?.

Sind Sie verheiratet?/Bist du verheiratet? - This is used to ask someone if they are married, it means Are you married?.

Haben Sie Kinder?/Hast du Kinder? - This is used to ask someone if they have children, it means Do you have children?.

How are you?/Wie geht's?

Wie geht es Ihnen? - This is the formal form of asking someone how are they, it means How are you?.

Wie geht es dir?/Wie geht's? - This is the informal form of asking someone how are they, it means the same as Wie geht es Ihnen.

Mir geht es gut! - This is how you would answer if you are fine, but if you are felling yourself way to well you can say Mir geht es sehr gut!.

gut/ sehr gut / super / wunderbar / ausgezeichnet / toll / prima / fantastisch - These are all positive answers you can get.

Es geht - If you are not felling well neither bad, you would say this, it means not too bad.

Mir geht es schlecht!/ Mir geht es nicht gut! - These means I'm not doing well.

Mir geht es sehr schlecht - This one is used when you are felling very bad, it means I'm felling very bad.

Miserabel / Furchtbar - These are used to say you are felling bad also, they mean miserable and awful.

Ich bin müde - This means I'm tired.

Ich bin krank - This means I'm sick.

Und Ihnen?/ Und dir? - These are used as a reply after you said how are you felling like a and you?, the first one is formal and the second is informal.

Auch gut, danke! - This is basically a reply for the Und Ihnen?/Und dir?, this means I'm good too!.
