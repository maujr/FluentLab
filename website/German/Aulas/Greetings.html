Greetings
Begrüßungen

Most Common

Hallo - It's the most common greeting and it means hi or hello.

Guten Tag - It's another really common greeting and it means good day, it can be used any time of the day, but is good to mention that this is said when you are in a formal situation.

It's also really normal that in Germany you get a hand shake while meeting someone.

For certain parts of the day

Guten Morgen! - It's used in the morning like good morning which has the same meaning.

Guten Abend! - It's used in the evening like good evening which has the same meanin.

Gute Nacht! - It's used before going to sleep just like good night which has the same meaning.

Some other greetings

Grüß Gott! - This greeting is usually used in Austria and Bayern (Germany), and it means the same thing as hallo.

Grüß Sie! - This greeting is used in casual situations, it means the same as Grüß Gott, but Grüß Sie is a bit formal.

Grüß dich! - This one is also used in casual situations, it also means the same as Grüß Gott Grüß dich is more informal than Grüß Sie.

Servus! - This greeting is commonly used in Switzerland and southern Germany, and it can mean both hello and bye, and it can be used any time in the day.

Tschüs!/Tschüss! - This greeting basically means bye, and it's used all around Germany, and most of the times is informal. Ciao is also used, as in some other countries in Europe, but ciao comes from the italian language.

Auf Wiedersehen! - This greeting means see you again, and while talking in the phone wit someone, you would use auf wiederhören, which mean something like Until I hear you again or Until I talk to you again.

Bis Bald! - This greeting commonly used among friends, it means see you soon.

Bis Später! - This one is really similar to Bis Bald and it means see you later.

Common phrases

Danke!/Danke schön! - It basically mean thanks/thank you and if you are really grateful you can say vielen Dank.

Bitte!/Bitte schön! - It can be used as a reply to Dank, Danke schön and vielen Dank, but you can also use bitte to say please.

Entschuldigen sie, bitte - You can use this to say excuse me please, but this sentence is a bit to formal, in other situations, you can use instead Entschuldigung bitte, which means the same thing but is a lot more informal.

Entschuldigung/Es tut mir leid - You can use both of this expressions to say sorry.

Wie bitte?/noch einmal, bitte - You would use these sentences when you don't understand what someone is saying, but you can say Bitte wiederholen Sie, which is more formal.

Sprechen Sie Deutsch?/Sprechen Sie Englisch? - These questions asks respectively if you speak german and if you speak english.

Ich spreche kein Deutsch./Ich spreche kein Englisch. - If you don't speak german you will use the first sentence, and if you don't speak english you will say the second one.

Ich spreche etwas Deutsch./Ich spreche etwas Englisch. - You will use these to say that you speak a bit of german and speak a bit of english

Ich verstehe nicht. - It means I don't understand.

Ich weiß nicht. - It means I don't know.

Keine Ahnung. - It means no idea.

Kein Problem! - It means no problem.

Einen Moment, bitte! - It means Just a moment please.

Ja/Nein/Vielleicht - These word are, by far, the most important ones in the german language, because they mean respectively: yes, no and may be.
